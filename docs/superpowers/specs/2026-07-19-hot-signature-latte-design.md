# The B's Hot Signature Latte — Formula and Image Design

Date: 2026-07-19
Status: Approved direction; awaiting written-spec review

## Objective

Develop a repeatable hot signature latte for The B's Club using the existing 160 ml white ceramic cup and the shop's thick cream top. Create a photorealistic website-ready concept image that represents the approved drink accurately.

## Selected Concept

Product name: **The B's Salted Caramel Cloud Latte**

The drink should taste coffee-forward, lightly sweet and creamy without becoming heavy. The cream top is a small central accent that melts gradually into the hot latte; it must not cover the entire surface.

## Test Formula — One 160 ml Serving

- House espresso: one machine extraction yielding 50 ml
- Caramel sauce mixed into the coffee: 5–6 g
- Fine salt: 0.05–0.1 g
- Steamed whole milk: 75–85 g, adjusted to the cup's fill line
- Existing house cream top: 10–12 g
- Caramel garnish: approximately 1 g

## Preparation

1. Preheat the 160 ml cup.
2. Add 5–6 g caramel sauce and 0.05–0.1 g fine salt.
3. Run the shop's current espresso program once to yield 50 ml, then stir until uniform.
4. Steam the milk to 55–58°C with a thin, glossy microfoam.
5. Pour the milk, leaving approximately 1 cm below the rim for the topping.
6. Place 10–12 g of the existing thick cream top in the centre. Do not spread it edge to edge.
7. Finish with approximately 1 g caramel in a thin spiral.

## Service and Quality Criteria

- Espresso remains identifiable after the first sip.
- Sweetness is present but does not dominate the coffee.
- Cream top floats initially and softens gradually; it is not a spoon-only layer.
- The drink remains comfortable to drink directly from the cup.
- Staff can reproduce the drink using weighed portions.
- Use one extraction from the existing grinder and espresso setup; the recipe does not require a second grind or a double dose.
- The final recipe must be taste-tested in the actual cup before it is added to the public menu.

## Image Direction

Reference image role: preserve the real cup and saucer design, including the round handle, white glaze and proportions.

Create one photorealistic 3:2 landscape product image for website use:

- The full 160 ml cup and saucer remain visible with generous safe space on every side.
- Camera angle is slightly elevated so the drink surface is clearly visible.
- The latte has a warm caramel-brown surface, a small ivory cream-top dome and a fine caramel spiral.
- A subtle amount of natural steam communicates that the drink is hot.
- Background is a warm, softly blurred café setting consistent with The B's Club.
- Lighting is natural and premium, not glossy studio stock photography.
- No invented logo, text, watermark, spoon, biscuits or unavailable props.
- The topping must look edible and realistically scaled for a 160 ml cup.

## Image Acceptance Criteria

- Cup shape and white saucer remain faithful to the reference.
- Cup, handle and saucer are not cropped.
- The cream top does not resemble whipped cream piled high or ice cream.
- Caramel is restrained and does not drip messily over the cup.
- The image remains usable in both a desktop card and a mobile crop without cutting the cup.
- No visible AI artefacts, malformed ceramics or duplicated handles.

## Publishing Boundary

The generated image is a concept preview. It must not replace the live website image until the drink has been made and approved in the shop. The current CHF 4.90 price remains unchanged unless a separate costing and pricing decision is approved.
